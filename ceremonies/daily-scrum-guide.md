# Daily Scrum Stand‑up Guide  
Tips for running an effective Daily Scrum.

> Based on the original article:  
> https://ericspink.medium.com/tips-for-an-effective-daily-scrum-stand-up-3e69c6059f69

The Daily Scrum is the first ceremony of the day and sets the tone for the work ahead. When done well, it helps the team align, adapt, and plan effectively. When it goes stale, team members simply “go through the motions,” updates become unfocused, and the ceremony loses its purpose.

This guide outlines practical ways to keep the Daily Scrum focused, engaging, and valuable.

---

## 🧭 What the Daily Scrum Is  
The Scrum Guide describes the Daily Scrum as a 15‑minute event where Developers:

- Inspect progress toward the Sprint Goal  
- Adapt the Sprint Backlog  
- Create a plan for the next 24 hours  

The structure is flexible as long as the outcome is an actionable plan for the day. Daily Scrums improve communication, surface impediments, and reduce the need for additional meetings.

While the Scrum Guide provides best practices, real‑world teams may need slight adjustments based on size, technology, or experience. The goal is always to stay as close to the intent as possible.

---

## 🎯 Establish the Purpose and Ideal Outcome  
The Daily Scrum exists to help the team plan the day and stay aligned on the Sprint Goal. Without a clear purpose, the ceremony becomes a routine status meeting.

Reinforce the purpose regularly, especially when:

- Updates become vague  
- Team members drift into excessive detail  
- The Sprint Goal is not being referenced  
- The meeting feels like a formality  

Helpful guiding questions include:

- Did you face any blockers or risks yesterday?  
- What is the highest‑priority story you’re moving forward today?  
- Do you need help, or can you help someone else?  
- Do you foresee any risks in the next few days?  

These questions keep the conversation focused on outcomes, not activity.

---

## ⏰ Ensure Everyone Is On Time  
The Daily Scrum is short and time‑sensitive. When people arrive late:

- Important information is missed  
- Blockers go unnoticed  
- The meeting runs long  
- Other meetings are impacted  

If lateness becomes a pattern:

- Bring it up in the Retrospective  
- Discuss the impact without naming individuals  
- Have a casual, supportive conversation with anyone consistently late  
- Explore whether the time needs adjusting  

The goal is to maintain respect for everyone’s time.

---

## 😀 Start With Something Fun  
Since the Daily Scrum often starts the workday, a quick moment of positivity helps energize the team.

Ideas include:

- A one‑minute stretch or movement  
- A dad joke  
- A fun “holiday of the day”  
- A quick icebreaker question  

Keep it brief — no more than a minute — to avoid derailing the ceremony.

---

## 📋 Use the Sprint Board  
Visuals help everyone stay aligned. Whether using Jira, a digital board, or a physical board:

- Share the Active Sprint Board during the call  
- Stand around the physical board if on‑site  
- Encourage team members to reference the story cards directly  

A visible board reinforces:

- Sprint priorities  
- Story progress  
- Action items or experiments  
- Work distribution  

It also helps team members remember what they were working on, especially after weekends or holidays.

---

## ⏱️ Limit Time and Save Deep Discussions for After  
The Daily Scrum is not the place for:

- Detailed technical explanations  
- Problem‑solving conversations  
- One‑on‑one discussions  
- Full walkthroughs of yesterday’s work  

If updates become too detailed:

- Step in and refocus on the Sprint Goal  
- Redirect deeper conversations to after the meeting  
- Encourage team members to collaborate outside the ceremony  

When discussions run long, people tune out — and important information gets missed.

---

## 🚧 Everyone Listens for Hidden Blockers  
Different roles and skill sets allow team members to spot risks others may miss. Hidden blockers often surface when:

- Someone describes unexpected complexity  
- A story is stuck in the same state for days  
- A team member sounds uncertain or overwhelmed  

Encourage the entire team to listen actively and call out potential blockers. This builds shared ownership and helps less experienced members learn what to watch for.

---

## 🔄 Share Who Leads  
Having the same person lead every day can make the ceremony feel repetitive. Rotating leadership:

- Keeps the meeting fresh  
- Encourages engagement  
- Builds facilitation skills  
- Helps team members understand the Sprint more deeply  

Rotate daily or weekly — whatever fits the team best.

---

## 📉 Show or Review the Burndown Chart  
The Burndown chart provides a quick snapshot of Sprint progress. Use it to:

- Display progress as people join the call  
- Highlight scope creep  
- Identify slowdowns or stalled stories  
- Discuss trends later in the Sprint  

If the chart shows little movement, stories may be too large. If it trends upward, scope is being added and should be addressed.

---

## 🔍 Hold a Mid‑Sprint Check‑In  
A deeper mid‑Sprint review helps determine whether the team needs to pivot. This is separate from the Daily Scrum and may require extra time.

A strong mid‑Sprint check‑in includes:

1. A snapshot of Sprint progress  
2. A review of the Burndown chart  
3. A look at work distribution across swim lanes  
4. A walkthrough of priority stories (not by person)  
5. Questions such as:  
   - Are there blockers on this story?  
   - Is it at risk?  
   - Do we need to swarm?  
   - Does someone need help?  

The goal is a clear plan for the remainder of the Sprint.

---

## 🎮 Play a Game Early in the Sprint  
The first day of a Sprint often doesn’t require a full Daily Scrum — priorities were set the day before, and work is just beginning. This is a great opportunity to build team morale.

Quick, inclusive games include:

- **Gartic Phone**  
- **Codenames**  
- **Three‑Minute Animal**  

Keep it light and brief to avoid disrupting the flow of the Sprint.

---

## 🏁 Conclusion  
A strong Daily Scrum keeps the team aligned, focused, and ready to tackle the day. By reinforcing purpose, keeping updates concise, using visuals, rotating leadership, and addressing blockers early, the ceremony becomes a powerful tool for achieving the Sprint Goal.
