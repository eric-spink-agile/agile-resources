# Agile Team Leaders Connect  
Staying aligned as a Product Owner, Team Lead, and Scrum Master.

> Based on the original article:  
> https://ericspink.medium.com/agile-team-leaders-connect-staying-aligned-with-your-product-owner-team-lead-and-scrum-master-ad8888f9cee0

Strong Agile teams have multiple leaders — each with different responsibilities, perspectives, and priorities. The Product Owner focuses on value and stakeholders, the Technical Lead represents the developers, and the Scrum Master protects the team and guides Agile practices. When these leaders are aligned, the team moves confidently and efficiently. When they’re not, confusion and mixed messages follow.

A weekly Agile Team Leaders Connect creates a dedicated space for alignment, clarity, and shared decision‑making.

---

## 🎯 Establish the Purpose and Ideal Outcome  
Meetings without clear objectives quickly become unfocused. The Agile Team Leaders Connect should have a well‑defined purpose and expected outcomes so leaders know why the meeting exists and what it should achieve.

Include in the meeting invite:

- A clear description of the connect  
- The ideal outcome  
- A link to the shared agenda  
- A place to track action items  
- A shared chat or communication channel  

At the first session, clarify that this is a leaders‑only meeting where anything can be discussed — from upcoming Retrospective topics to product direction, technical debt priorities, or team challenges. Sensitive items may arise, so confidentiality and trust are essential.

The ideal outcome is alignment across all leadership roles before communicating with the team.

---

## 📝 Share an Open Agenda  
The connect works best as an open forum where all three leaders contribute topics. A shared document keeps the agenda transparent and helps everyone prepare.

A simple online document is enough. Each leader can add topics throughout the week, and the group can review past discussions when needed.

Tools that work well:

- Google Docs  
- Trello  
- Slack Conversation 

An open agenda ensures the meeting stays collaborative and relevant.

---

## 🗂️ Use a Visual Kanban Board  
Just like the team’s work, leadership action items benefit from visibility and flow. A Kanban board helps track:

- Ideas  
- Improvements  
- Decisions  
- Action items  
- Progress over time  

Set up columns such as:

- **To Do**  
- **In Progress**  
- **Done**  
- Optional: **Future Ideas**, **Blocked**, etc.

Discuss the board at each connect. This creates accountability, shows progress, and provides a record of what leadership has accomplished to support the team.

Tools that work well:

- ClickUp  
- Trello  
- Jira 

A Kanban board can also act as a roadmap for leadership improvements.

---

## 📅 Schedule Weekly and Align With the Sprint  
The connect should happen weekly, ideally at consistent points in the Sprint cycle.

For two‑week Sprints:

- **Week 1 (mid‑Sprint):**  
  Review ongoing work, discuss experiments, identify risks, and determine if a pivot is needed.

- **Week 2 (late‑Sprint):**  
  Align on Sprint outcomes, upcoming priorities, Retrospective topics, and preparation for the next Sprint.

Timing the connect around key Sprint moments ensures leaders stay aligned when it matters most.

---

## 👥 Gather Only the Leaders  
The connect should follow the MVP mindset — only the essential people should attend. Limiting the meeting to the Product Owner, Technical Lead, and Scrum Master ensures:

- Sensitive topics can be discussed openly  
- Time isn’t wasted for non‑leaders  
- Decisions remain at the team level  
- Autonomy is preserved  

Including senior leaders or additional team members can dilute the purpose and reduce psychological safety. This meeting exists to align the three roles that guide the team day‑to‑day.

---

## 🏁 Conclusion  
The Agile Team Leaders Connect strengthens alignment across the Product Owner, Technical Lead, and Scrum Master. When these leaders share context, direction, and priorities, the team gains clarity and confidence. Strong leadership alignment leads to stronger teams, better decisions, and smoother delivery.
